# Final Project Description: Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning

This final project for Reinforcement Learning explores and compares various multi-agent reinforcement learning algorithms across two distinct phases, investigating the effectiveness of tabular versus deep learning approaches in cooperative multi-agent environments. In **Checkpoint 1**, we implemented and compared **Independent Q-Learning (IQL)** and **Cooperative Q-Learning (CQL)** using tabular methods on a custom multi-agent warehouse environment (`MultiAgentWarehouseEnv`) featuring a 6×6 grid with obstacles, where two agents must navigate to pickup locations, collect packages, and deliver them to dropoff zones while avoiding collisions. The IQL approach maintained separate Q-tables for each agent, allowing independent learning but potentially missing cooperative opportunities, while CQL utilized a shared joint Q-table that explicitly modeled the joint action space (36 possible joint actions for 2 agents), enabling the learning of coordinated strategies. After training IQL for 15,000 episodes and CQL for 20,000 episodes with carefully tuned hyperparameters (learning rates of 0.1 and 0.12 respectively, discount factor of 0.98, and exponential epsilon decay), evaluation results demonstrated that CQL achieved a perfect 100% success rate with highly stable performance (reward standard deviation of 11.31), significantly outperforming IQL's 90% success rate with much higher variance (standard deviation of 163.38), clearly illustrating the advantages of explicit cooperation modeling in multi-agent settings.

In the **Final Project**, we extended our investigation to deep reinforcement learning methods, implementing **Independent Deep Q-Networks (IDQN)** and **Double DQN (DDQN)** to handle more complex, continuous state spaces. We developed a custom `WarehouseParallelEnv` using PettingZoo's parallel interface, featuring a 6×6 grid with 3 agents, each with their own randomly assigned pickup and dropoff locations, and also experimented with PettingZoo's `simple_spread_v3` environment from the Multi-Agent Particle Environment (MPE) suite, where 3 agents must spread out to cover 3 landmarks. Our deep learning implementation utilized PyTorch-based neural networks with 2-layer MLPs (256×256 hidden units), experience replay buffers (ranging from 200,000 to 350,000 transitions), target networks updated periodically (every 1,000-2,000 steps), and epsilon-greedy exploration with linear decay schedules. For the warehouse environment, IDQN was trained for 800 episodes with a learning rate of 3e-4, achieving only a 1.38% average success rate with high reward variance (-375.57 ± 246.24), highlighting the challenges of deep multi-agent learning. On the MPE Simple Spread environment, we trained IDQN for 1,500 episodes and DDQN for 1,800 episodes, where DDQN addressed Q-value overestimation by decoupling action selection (using the policy network) from action evaluation (using the target network). While both methods struggled to achieve task success (0% success rate for both), DDQN demonstrated improved training stability with slightly lower reward variance (-77.21 ± 24.51 vs -77.24 ± 26.17 for IDQN), suggesting that the Double DQN modification provides benefits even when overall performance remains challenging. Throughout the project, we implemented comprehensive logging, visualization (generating training curves for rewards and success rates), and result persistence (saving experiment results as pickle files), enabling thorough analysis of algorithm performance. The key findings reveal that while tabular cooperative methods excel in discrete, small-scale environments (CQL's 100% success rate), deep multi-agent reinforcement learning faces significant challenges including sample inefficiency, exploration difficulties, and the non-stationarity introduced by simultaneously learning agents, necessitating more advanced techniques such as centralized training with decentralized execution (CTDE), value decomposition networks (VDN/QMIX), or actor-critic methods (MADDPG) for effective learning in complex multi-agent scenarios.
